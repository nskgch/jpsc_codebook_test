Respondent identification number¶

Variable name: ID¶

Description¶

It is the unique number assigned to each respondent. The number also enables identification of the time of the initial survey and the birth cohort.

ID: 1-1500¶

  • Sample taken for the first survey (1993) (cohort A)
  • Women aged 24 to 34 years at the time of the initial survey (October 1, 1993) (age range: 11years)
  • Born in the period of October 2, 1958 to October 1, 1968
  • Initial sample size: 1,500 people

ID: 2001-2500¶

  • Sample taken for the fifth survey (1997) (cohort B)
  • Women aged 24 to 27 years at the time of the initial survey (October 1, 1997) (age range: 4years)
  • Born in the period of October 2, 1969 to October 1, 1972
  • Initial sample size: 500 people

ID: 3001-3838¶

  • Sample taken for the eleventh survey (2003) (cohort C)
  • Woman aged 24 to 29 years at the time of the initial survey (October 1, 2003) (age range: 6years)
  • Born in the period of October 2, 1973 to October 1, 1978
  • Initial sample size: 836 people
  • Missing IDs: 3178 and 3403

ID: 4001-4645¶

  • Sample taken for the sixteenth survey (2008) (cohort D)
  • Women aged 24 to 28 years at the time of the initial survey (October 1, 2013) (age range: 5 years)
  • Born in the period of October 2, 1985 to October 1, 1989
  • Initial sample size: 636 people
  • Missing IDs: 4342, 4343, 4401, 4407, 4435, 4447, 4448, 4515, and 4644

ID: 5001-5648¶

  • Sample taken for the twenty-first survey (2013) (Cohort E)
  • Woman aged 24 to 28 years at the time of the initial survey (October 1, 2013) (age range: 5 years)
  • Born in the period of October 2, 1985 to October 1, 1989
  • Initial sample size: 648 people
